Understanding Home Affordability

Lenders typically use the 28/36 rule to determine home affordability. Your housing payment (including principal, interest, taxes, and insurance) should not exceed 28% of your gross monthly income, and your total debt payments should stay below 36%.

Your debt-to-income (DTI) ratio is crucial for mortgage approval. A lower DTI gives you better loan terms and interest rates. Most lenders prefer DTI below 43%, though some programs allow higher ratios with compensating factors like larger down payments.

While lenders may approve you for a certain amount, consider your comfort level and other financial goals. Save 3-6 months of expenses, factor in maintenance costs (1-2% of home value annually), and don't forget closing costs (2-5% of purchase price).
