HOW MUCH DO YOU NEED TO RETIRE?

A guide to finding your "Magic Number" for financial independence.

The "Magic Number" Myth

There is no single dollar amount that fits everyone. For some, $500,000 is plenty; for others, $5 million isn't enough. Your retirement goal depends entirely on your expected lifestyle and expenses.

Financial experts generally suggest you aim to replace 70% to 90% of your pre-retirement income to maintain your current standard of living.

The 4% Rule: A Benchmark for Success

One of the most popular strategies is the 4% Rule. It suggests that if you withdraw 4% of your total retirement savings in your first year of retirement (and adjust for inflation thereafter), your money should last at least 30 years.

Age-Based Savings Milestones

Are you on track? While every journey is different, Fidelity and other major institutions suggest these salary-based milestones:

  • By Age 30: Have 1x your annual salary saved.
  • By Age 40: Have 3x your annual salary saved.
  • By Age 50: Have 6x your annual salary saved.
  • By Age 60: Have 8x your annual salary saved.
  • By Age 67: Have 10x your annual salary saved.

The Three Pillars of Retirement Income

Don't rely on just one bucket. A secure retirement usually stands on three legs:

  • Social Security: Think of this as your floor, not the whole building. It typically replaces about 40% of an average worker's income.
  • Personal Savings (401k/IRA): These are your primary growth engines. Take advantage of employer matches!
  • Investments/Pensions: Real estate, dividend stocks, or traditional pensions provide the "ceiling" for a comfortable lifestyle.

Hidden Costs to Consider

When calculating your needs, don't forget the "Retirement Killers":

  • Healthcare: Many retirees underestimate the cost of Medicare premiums and out-of-pocket expenses.
  • Inflation: A dollar today won't buy as much in 30 years. Your plan must account for rising prices.
  • Taxes: Unless you are using a Roth account, Uncle Sam will want a piece of your withdrawals.
