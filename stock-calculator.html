About Stock Investing

Stock investing involves buying shares of publicly traded companies with the goal of generating returns through capital appreciation and dividends. Capital gains occur when you sell stocks for more than you paid, while dividends are periodic payments companies make to shareholders from profits.

Consider your investment timeline, risk tolerance, and diversification strategy. Long-term investing (1+ years) qualifies for lower capital gains tax rates. Dollar-cost averaging, buying regularly regardless of price, can reduce the impact of market volatility. Always research companies thoroughly and consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized guidance.
