Understanding Stock Returns

Stock returns come from two sources: capital gains (increase in stock price) and dividends (company profit distributions). Total return combines both and is the true measure of investment performance. A stock that increases 50% in price with 10% dividends provides a 60% total return.

Dividend reinvestment significantly boosts long-term returns through compound growth. Instead of taking dividends as cash, reinvesting purchases more shares, which generate their own dividends. Over decades, reinvested dividends can account for 30-40% of total stock market returns.

Annualized return shows your average yearly performance, making it easier to compare investments held for different periods. An investment that doubles in 7 years has an annualized return of about 10%. Remember to factor in taxes - long-term capital gains (held over 1 year) are taxed more favorably than short-term gains or regular income.
