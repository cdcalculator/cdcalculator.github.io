Understanding 401(k) Plans

A 401(k) is an employer-sponsored retirement plan that allows you to save and invest pre-tax dollars. Contributions reduce your taxable income, and investments grow tax-deferred until withdrawal in retirement. Many employers offer matching contributions, which is essentially free money.

Employer matching is powerful. A common match is 50% of your contributions up to 6% of salary. If you contribute 6%, your employer adds 3%, giving you a 50% instant return on investment. Always contribute enough to get the full match.

For 2024, contribution limits are $23,000 ($30,500 if age 50+). Start early to maximize compound growth - investing $500/month starting at age 25 can grow to over $1.5 million by age 65 with 8% returns. Even small increases in contributions can significantly impact your retirement nest egg.
