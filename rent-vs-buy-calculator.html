About Rent vs Buy Decision

The rent vs buy decision depends on many factors beyond pure finances, including lifestyle flexibility, job stability, local market conditions, and personal goals. Buying builds equity but comes with maintenance costs, property taxes, and less flexibility. Renting offers flexibility and no maintenance responsibilities but no equity building.

Consider the break-even point: how long until buying becomes more cost-effective than renting. In high-cost areas or if you plan to move soon, renting may be better. For long-term stability in appreciating markets, buying often wins. Factor in closing costs, maintenance (typically 1% of home value annually), and opportunity cost of your down payment.
