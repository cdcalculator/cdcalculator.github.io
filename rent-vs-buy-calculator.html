Renting vs Buying Decision

The rent vs buy decision depends on many factors beyond just monthly costs. Buying builds equity and provides tax benefits, but requires a substantial down payment and comes with maintenance costs, property taxes, and less flexibility to relocate.

Renting offers flexibility, predictable monthly costs, and no maintenance responsibilities. You avoid the risks of declining home values and can invest your down payment elsewhere. However, rent increases over time and you build no equity.

Generally, buying makes more sense if you plan to stay 5+ years, have stable income, and can afford 20% down. Renting is better for those prioritizing flexibility, living in expensive markets, or still building their down payment. Consider your career plans, market conditions, and personal financial goals.
