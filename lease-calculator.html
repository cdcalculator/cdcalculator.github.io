How to Use the Lease Calculator

  1. Enter the vehicle's MSRP (sticker price)
  2. Input your down payment and trade-in value if applicable
  3. Specify the lease term in months (typically 24, 36, or 48)
  4. Enter the money factor provided by the dealer
  5. Input the residual value percentage (usually 50-65%)
  6. Add your local sales tax rate
  7. Click Calculate to see your monthly payment and total cost

About Car Leasing

Leasing a car means paying for the vehicle's depreciation during the lease term rather than the full purchase price. The money factor is similar to an interest rate (multiply by 2,400 to convert to APR), and the residual value represents what the car will be worth at lease end.

Leasing typically requires lower monthly payments than buying, but you don't build equity. Consider factors like annual mileage limits, wear-and-tear charges, and whether you prefer driving a new car every few years versus long-term ownership.
