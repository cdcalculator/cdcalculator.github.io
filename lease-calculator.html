How to Use the Lease Calculator

  1. Enter the vehicle's MSRP (manufacturer's suggested retail price)
  2. Input the negotiated sale price (capitalized cost)
  3. Add your down payment or trade-in value
  4. Enter the residual value percentage (usually 50-65%)
  5. Input the money factor provided by the dealer
  6. Select your lease term and add local sales tax rate
  7. Click "Calculate" to see your monthly payment

Understanding Car Leases

A car lease is essentially a long-term rental where you pay for the vehicle's depreciation during the lease term, plus interest (expressed as a money factor) and fees. You don't own the car at the end; you simply return it or have the option to buy it at the residual value.

The money factor is the interest rate used in leasing. To convert it to APR, multiply by 2,400. For example, a money factor of 0.00125 equals 3% APR. Lower money factors mean lower financing costs and better lease deals.

Leasing works best if you drive less than 12,000-15,000 miles per year, want lower monthly payments, and enjoy driving a new car every few years. However, you'll never build equity, and excess mileage or wear-and-tear charges can be costly.
