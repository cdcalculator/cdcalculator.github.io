Understanding APR

Annual Percentage Rate (APR) represents the true yearly cost of a loan, including both the interest rate and all associated fees. While the interest rate only reflects the cost of borrowing the principal, APR provides a complete picture by incorporating origination fees, closing costs, and other charges.

APR is higher than the interest rate when fees are involved, making it a more accurate tool for comparing loan offers. Two loans with the same interest rate can have vastly different APRs depending on their fee structures.

When comparing loans, always look at the APR rather than just the interest rate. A loan with a slightly higher interest rate but lower fees may have a better APR and cost less overall. Federal law requires lenders to disclose APR, helping consumers make informed borrowing decisions.
