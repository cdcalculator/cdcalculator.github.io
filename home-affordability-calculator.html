About Home Affordability

The 28/36 rule is a common guideline: your housing payment shouldn't exceed 28% of gross monthly income, and total debt shouldn't exceed 36%. Lenders use debt-to-income (DTI) ratios to determine loan approval, with most requiring DTI below 43%.

Consider all costs including principal, interest, property taxes, insurance (PITI), HOA fees, maintenance, and utilities. A conservative approach ensures you can comfortably afford your home while maintaining financial flexibility for other goals and emergencies.
