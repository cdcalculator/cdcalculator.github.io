SNOWBALL VS. AVALANCHE METHOD

A deep dive into the math and psychology of becoming debt-free.

The Debt Dilemma

When you're facing multiple balances—credit cards, student loans, or medical bills—knowing where to start is often the hardest part. Two main philosophies dominate the world of debt repayment: The Debt Snowball and the Debt Avalanche.

While one focuses on mathematical efficiency, the other focuses on psychological momentum. Understanding which one fits your personality is the key to staying consistent until the balance hits zero.

The Debt Snowball: Psychological Wins

The Snowball method ignores interest rates. Instead, you list your debts from smallest balance to largest balance. You pay the minimum on everything except the smallest debt, which you attack with every extra dollar you have.

  • How it works: You pay off the smallest debt quickly, giving you a "win."
  • The benefit: Seeing a debt disappear entirely provides a massive psychological boost.
  • The downside: You might pay more in interest over time because you're ignoring the rates.

The Debt Avalanche: Mathematical Prowess

The Avalanche method prioritizes interest rates. You list your debts from the highest interest rate to the lowest, regardless of the balance size. You attack the most expensive debt first.

  • How it works: You eliminate the debt that is costing you the most money in interest every day.
  • The benefit: Mathematically, this is the fastest way to get out of debt and saves you the most money.
  • The downside: If your highest-interest debt is a large balance, it may take months or years to feel like you're making progress.

Comparison at a Glance

Feature Snowball Avalanche

Which One Should You Choose?

If you are easily discouraged and need to see progress to stay motivated, the Snowball is for you. However, if you are disciplined and motivated by the numbers, the Avalanche will save you more money in the long run.

Remember: The best method is the one you will actually stick to until the end.
