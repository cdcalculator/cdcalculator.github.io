THE MAGIC OF COMPOUND INTEREST

Why time is your greatest asset in wealth building.

What is Compound Interest?

Compound interest is the interest you earn on both your original money and the interest you keep accumulating. Unlike simple interest, which only calculates a percentage of your principal, compounding creates a "snowball effect" where your money grows at an accelerating rate.

Albert Einstein famously called it the "eighth wonder of the world." Those who understand it, earn it; those who don't, pay it.

The Three Pillars of Compounding

  • Time: The longer you leave your money alone, the steeper the growth curve becomes.
  • Rate of Return: Even a 1% or 2% difference in interest rates can result in hundreds of thousands of dollars over 30 years.
  • Consistency: Adding small amounts regularly (monthly or yearly) feeds the engine of growth.

See it in Action

Imagine you invest $10,000 at a 7% annual return:

  • After 10 years: You have $19,671
  • After 20 years: You have $38,696
  • After 40 years: You have $149,744

Notice how the growth between year 20 and 40 is nearly quadruple the growth of the first 20 years. That is the power of time.
