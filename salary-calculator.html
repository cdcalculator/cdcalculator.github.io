Understanding Salary Calculations

Annual salary is typically based on 2,080 hours per year (40 hours Ã— 52 weeks). To convert hourly to annual, multiply your hourly rate by 2,080. For annual to hourly, divide by 2,080. Remember that this is gross pay before taxes and deductions.

Take-home pay is your actual income after taxes and deductions. Federal income tax, Social Security (6.2%), Medicare (1.45%), state tax, and local taxes reduce your gross salary by 20-35% on average. Higher earners pay more due to progressive tax brackets.

When comparing job offers, look beyond base salary to total compensation. Include health insurance (worth $7,000-$20,000), 401(k) matching (typically 3-6% of salary), paid time off, bonuses, stock options, and other benefits. A $70,000 salary with excellent benefits may be worth more than an $80,000 salary with minimal benefits.
