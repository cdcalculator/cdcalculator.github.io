About Return on Investment

ROI (Return on Investment) is a performance measure used to evaluate the efficiency and profitability of an investment. It's calculated as: ROI = (Final Value - Total Investment) / Total Investment Ã— 100.

A positive ROI indicates profit, while negative ROI shows a loss. The annualized return normalizes ROI over time, making it easier to compare investments with different time horizons. For example, a 50% ROI over 5 years is different from 50% over 1 year.

Always include all costs in your ROI calculation, including transaction fees, management fees, taxes, and opportunity costs. This gives you a true picture of investment performance.
