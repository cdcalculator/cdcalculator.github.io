About Income Taxes

Income tax is calculated on your taxable income, which is your gross income minus deductions. The U.S. uses a progressive tax system with marginal tax brackets, meaning different portions of your income are taxed at different rates.

Standard deductions vary by filing status: Single ($13,850), Married Filing Jointly ($27,700), and Head of Household ($20,800) for 2023. You can choose standard or itemized deductions, whichever is higher.

Your effective tax rate is the average rate you pay on all your income, which is lower than your marginal rate. Understanding your tax liability helps with financial planning and retirement savings strategies.
