About Debt-to-Income Ratio

Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio measures the percentage of your gross monthly income that goes toward debt payments. Lenders use DTI to assess your ability to manage monthly payments and repay borrowed money.

Front-end DTI includes only housing costs (mortgage, taxes, insurance), while back-end DTI includes all monthly debt obligations. Most lenders prefer a back-end DTI below 43%, with 36% or lower considered excellent.

Improving your DTI involves increasing income or decreasing debt. Pay off high-interest debts first, avoid taking on new debt, and consider increasing your income through side jobs or career advancement.
