5 TIPS FOR FIRST-TIME HOMEBUYERS

Mastering the path from renting to owning without the financial stress.

1. Check Your Credit Score Early

Your credit score is the single most important factor in determining your mortgage interest rate. Even a 50-point difference can save you tens of thousands of dollars over the life of a 30-year loan.

Start monitoring your score at least 6-12 months before you plan to buy. Avoid opening new credit cards or taking out large auto loans during this period.

2. Save for More Than the Down Payment

Many buyers focus solely on the 3%, 5%, or 20% down payment. However, "closing costs" usually range from 2% to 5% of the home's purchase price. This covers inspections, appraisals, title insurance, and taxes.

Additionally, you should keep an "Emergency Maintenance Fund" ready. Unlike renting, when the water heater breaks in your own home, the bill is yours to pay.

3. Get Pre-Approved, Not Just Pre-Qualified

In a competitive market, a pre-approval letter is your ticket to being taken seriously by sellers. While pre-qualification is a basic estimate, pre-approval involves a lender verifying your income, taxes, and assets.

This gives you a concrete budget, so you don't fall in love with a house you literally cannot afford to buy.

4. Consider the "Total Monthly Cost"

Your mortgage principal and interest are only part of the story. When calculating affordability, always include:

  • Property Taxes: These vary wildly by neighborhood.
  • Homeowners Insurance: Essential for protecting your investment.
  • HOA Fees: If the home is in a managed community.
  • Utilities: Houses are often more expensive to heat and cool than apartments.

5. Think About Resale Value

Even though you're just moving in, you should think about moving out. Is the home in a good school district? Is it near public transit? These "externalities" ensure that your investment grows in value, making it easier to sell or upgrade in the future.
