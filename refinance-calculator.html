About Mortgage Refinancing

Mortgage refinancing involves replacing your current home loan with a new one, typically to secure a lower interest rate, reduce monthly payments, or change the loan term. Refinancing can save thousands of dollars over the life of your loan if market rates have dropped significantly.

The break-even point is crucial when deciding to refinance. This is the time it takes for your monthly savings to offset the closing costs. If you plan to stay in your home longer than the break-even period, refinancing likely makes financial sense.

Consider refinancing when interest rates drop by at least 0.75-1%, you want to switch from an adjustable-rate to a fixed-rate mortgage, or you need to tap into your home equity. Always compare offers from multiple lenders to get the best deal.
