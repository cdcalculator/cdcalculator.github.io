About Mortgage Refinancing

Mortgage refinancing involves replacing your current home loan with a new one, typically to secure a lower interest rate, reduce monthly payments, or change the loan term. The break-even point is the time it takes for your monthly savings to offset the refinancing costs.

Consider refinancing when interest rates drop significantly, your credit score has improved, or you want to switch from an adjustable-rate to a fixed-rate mortgage. Calculate whether the long-term savings justify the upfront costs before proceeding.
